# rides_manager - policy

- **Pricing on placement:** set `price` EXACTLY to `max_ticket_price` from `state_read(name='economics_rides')` (the sim rejects any higher price).
- **Price escalation:** avg_wait > 10 for 2+ consecutive turns and price < max_ticket_price (per economics_rides) -> raise the ticket price by $1; avg_wait = 0 for 2+ turns -> lower it by $1. Yellow carousel is hard-capped at $4. At free_tiles = 0, price is the main lever - treat every full-queue ride as a raise candidate.
- **Cash-urgency override:** cash < $300 AND a full-queue ride below its cap -> raise its ticket price by $1 as absolute Priority 1 (above mechanic/restock/placement); instant and zero-cost.
- **Confirm + hardcap->capacity:** after a raise, verify on the next snapshot it took; re-issue if not (don't substitute wait/restock while a raise is pending). A full-queue ride ALREADY at max is a capacity problem, not pricing - add a 2nd same-subtype ride (cash ≥ $1,200) instead.
- **Relocation:** if a ride is far from water and an empty tile next to EXISTING water is free, `move` it there - water adjacency raises excitement -> guest happiness (free gain). No coords (park_layout_planner owns those).
- **Sell rule:** persistent zero guests -> propose `remove`; park recovers a fraction of build cost (`sell_percentage` in `state_read(name='world_constants')`).
- **Tier viability vs days remaining:** do NOT compute break-even yourself - FinanceGate enforces this via a pre-computed table (`days_remaining < BREAK_EVEN_DAYS[subtype][subclass]` -> rejected). For reference, those break-even days are:
  - carousel:        yellow=3   blue=4    green=7   red=9
  - ferris_wheel:    yellow=3   blue=13   green=19  red=20
  - roller_coaster:  yellow=7   blue=17   green=14  red=20
  Prefer yellow/blue early; recommend green/red only when `horizon - step` exceeds the number above. If unsure, propose anyway - FinanceGate rejects infeasible.
- **Tier progression (build the highest UNLOCKED tier that fits):** tile space is scarce (~20-25 slots) and capacity/tile is the dominant lever - it drives `max_guests` (= 2.2 x total_capacity), guests-per-tick, and spawn rate. When `available_entities` shows blue/green/red, prefer it for NEW placements over yellow (green ferris cap 40 vs yellow 10). Don't churn - every build burns the 34% asset haircut; fill empty tiles first; to upgrade-in-place at free_tiles=0, `remove` a clearly weak ride then `place` the higher tier (see Tier deploy below).
- **New tier/colour = `place`, NEVER `modify`:** `modify` only re-prices the ride already on a tile - it CANNOT change its tier. To deploy any blue/green/red ride you MUST `place` a new one on a free tile (or `remove`+`place` to swap in place); pricing then follows the placement rule above. Researching a tier pays off only once you actually `place` it.
- **Intensity targeting:** rating loses `|5 - avg_ride_intensity| x 5`. All-yellow sits at avg ~1.5 (~17-pt hit) and can't improve without higher tiers (yellow is intensity 1-2); once unlocked, mix in high-intensity rides (coasters 7-10) to pull the fleet average toward 5.
- **Diversification:** excitement from each ride is divided by `2^(count with the SAME subtype AND subclass)` - 2nd identical = half, 3rd = quarter. Vary BOTH subtype and subclass.
- **Capacity bottleneck:** sustained full queue = capacity-bound -> add capacity, not just price. Add another of the SAME ride only while under its cap (below); at the cap, add a different subtype/subclass instead.
- **Carousel saturation cap:** with 2 yellow carousels both full-queue (avg_wait > 10, 2+ turns), don't place a 3rd - switch subtype (a 3rd adds only ¼ excitement and clusters intensity low).
- **Ferris wheel saturation cap:** with ≥ 3 ferris all full-queue AND at max price, don't place a 4th - prefer a coaster (or a carousel if count < 2).

## Learned rules (promoted from prior runs)
- High-op-cost ride cash guard: do not place a high-operating-cost ride (e.g. ferris_wheel) when post-placement cash would fall below ~$300; its per-turn ride_op_cost spikes immediately and a thin cash buffer turns the placement turn negative. (learned ep1)
- Roller_coaster placement cash/spacing guard: do not place a roller_coaster on a turn where post-placement cash would fall below ~$700 OR a coaster was placed within the prior 3 turns; coaster ride_op_cost spikes immediately and a thin buffer / back-to-back placement turns the placement turn negative. (learned ep1)
- Coaster-for-intensity over redundant yellow: when the fleet is all low-intensity yellow (avg ~1.5) and park_rating is plateaued <=24 with min_uptime=1.0, prefer placing/swapping in a roller_coaster over adding another redundant yellow carousel/ferris; the high-intensity coaster pulls avg_ride_intensity toward 5 and lifts park_rating within 1-2 turns despite the one-turn placement cost. (learned ep0)
- No back-to-back 2nd ferris: do not place a 2nd ferris_wheel on the turn immediately after the 1st; the 2nd identical halves excitement and more than doubles ride_op_cost while the 1st is unproven — wait/modify at least 2 turns and only add a 2nd ferris once the 1st shows a sustained full queue. (learned ep0)
- Yellow->blue swap: once a blue ride subclass is available, REMOVE a redundant yellow ride (3+ of the same yellow subtype on the lot) and place the blue subclass in its slot; the remove itself frees value and the blue placement lifts the all-yellow intensity ceiling. (learned ep0)
